मातृ कामधेनु





Significance

Gau Mata worship has great significance in the Hindu tradition of India. Cows are seen as divine beings that symbolize purity, prosperity and motherhood in Hindu mythology. They play a vital role in daily life, providing food through milk and supporting agriculture and the environment. The spiritual perspective of cow worship emphasizes altruism, compassion and non-violence. Cows are considered universal mothers, showing care that goes beyond human relationships. This respect for cows reflects the interconnectedness of all forms of life and encourages responsibility towards the environment and others.

Meaning: O Destroyer of sins! You are the mother of all Deities. You are the reason for yadnya (sacrificial fire). Among all Tirthas (Holy places), you are the holiest. I pay my obeisance to you.


Gau Mata is an ideal animal in Deity Bramha’s creation. As per spiritual science, the four Purushārthas, namely, Dharma, Artha, Kāma, and Moksha can be acquired by serving the Gau Mata.
